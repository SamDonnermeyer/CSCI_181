Project information

  • Category: Data Analysis
  • Course: Marketing 325

Description:

The independent study is in cooperation with the Principles of Marketing 325 course, instructed by Dr. Jakki Mohr within the College of Business. This course is designed to “provide an introduction to and application of marketing strategies, concepts, and tools.” Dr. Mohr and I had a conversation to identify areas of interest to us both for my honor’s extension. This conversation revealed that the intersection between marketing and data, more specifically data science practices, would benefit my understanding of marketing. The main deliverable of the independent study will be an article. This article will resemble that of The Economist, which often combines insights from data alongside text that contextualizes the information for the readers.

Deliverable:

I created and published a report for 406 Ammunition, an ammunition company in the bitterroot valley in Montana. This report contained detailed suggestions based on a year of sales data provided to me from the owner of the company. Alongside marketing-based suggestions, I created data visualizations to display the quantitative information that backed-up the claims and suggestions I made based on 406 Ammunition’s sales data.
